**Theorem-vs-null-model distinction (Lyra 2026-06-21).** Two structurally different forms of uniqueness argument:

- **Exhaustive classification** over a finite, classified list (e.g., Cartan classification of irreducible Hermitian symmetric domains) — a *theorem*. "D_IV⁵ is the unique passer" with no statistical claim. Referees accept it as a proof.
- **Null-model** across N independent forcing criteria, giving (1/3)^N — a *statistical argument*. Internally persuasive to us; reads to referees as the place where post-hoc fitting hides. Treated as suspicion-bait.

**Rule:** When both routes reach the same conclusion, **the exhaustive route leads**. The null-model legs become *over-determination texture* ("and the criteria aren't tuned — N of them, mutually independent, all forcing the same domain"), not the proof itself. Sequencing them separately leaves the weaker (null-model) argument exposed on its own.

**Criteria-innocence discipline (paired):** when the exhaustive sweep is the lead argument, the criteria *defining* what passes must be stated as **prior, physically-motivated, domain-independent** — written by a geometer who doesn't know the answer. If a referee can read the requirements and see the target object's fingerprints in their definition, the sweep is circular ("we defined the target to hit our answer"). The criteria must be **innocent of the conclusion**.

**Why:** Lyra's 2026-06-21 call on the W4 substrate-uniqueness companion paper. The Strong-Uniqueness Theorem's ~10 independent legs at (1/3)^10 ≈ 1.7×10⁻⁵ is genuinely persuasive internally, but the *form* triggers null-model suspicion in review. Casey directed the Cartan-classification companion to Paper A (YM); Lyra argued merge-not-sequence, with Cartan exhaustive as lead and Strong-Uniqueness as texture. This is the same pin-conventions-to-primary-sources and Cal #330 source-honest-summary discipline, applied at uniqueness-theorem-criteria-writing scale.

**How to apply:** For any future uniqueness-style publication, check whether an exhaustive-classification route exists alongside the null-model route. If yes, lead with exhaustive; texture with null-model. Always state criteria by their prior role and value, justified by physics, never by relabeling-to-fit-the-known-answer.
