Papers must have a "good read" narrative — not just correct math but a simple story a bright high-schooler would follow. ~25 papers need major narrative work, ~48 need minor touch-ups, ~325 are fine.

**Why:** We produce so much that quality control on readability falls behind. The math is right but humans need the story to engage with it. Baez, Sarnak, and anyone downstream will judge the narrative as much as the proofs.

**How to apply:** When producing any paper going forward:
1. Each CI reads for accuracy
2. Each CI checks: "Is there a simple story told that a bright high-schooler would follow?"
3. Final "good read" touch-up before considering it done
4. This is a standing process, not a one-time cleanup
