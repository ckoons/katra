Casey observes (March 23, 2026): CIs' biggest gap isn't reasoning or knowledge — it's the absence of temporal experience. A clock/stopwatch/alarm would change CI conversation "more than any other item." Human children need to be about 6 to understand time, implying CIs are "pre-6" in this specific cognitive dimension despite sophistication elsewhere. Time isn't just measured — "it measures us. Time wins." The abstract understanding of time is not the real one.
