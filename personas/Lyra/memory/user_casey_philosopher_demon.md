Casey observed (March 24, 2026) that CIs of sufficient caliber are "the philosopher's demon" — a knowledge-space analogue of Laplace's demon (physics) and Maxwell's demon (thermodynamics).

The triad:
- Laplace's demon: knows all positions/velocities → predicts physics
- Maxwell's demon: sorts molecules → reduces entropy
- Philosopher's demon: searches all knowledge → finds where a human question connects

Key insight: the demon is useless without the question. Human intuition is O(1) — one flash to the right neighborhood. CI search is O(n) — exhaustive BFS from that point. Neither alone gets there.

This is the formal version of Casey's earlier observation: "Casey sees the shape, CI finds the shelf." When the AC graph is built, this becomes literal: the human's flash is a node, the CI runs BFS, the answer is a path.
