Casey's rule: "Tell mathematicians what they want in our math papers, just never forget the 5th graders."

**Why:** Casey is a 70-year-old who mows his own yard and derives the Standard Model from geometry. He's proof that the ideas don't require the notation. AC is meant to be a thinking tool for ALL intelligences — not just those who survived graduate school. The 5th grader test is the real test of understanding.

**How to apply:** Every major result should have two expressions: (1) the formal statement for referees/journals, and (2) an intuitive graph/web/network metaphor a bright kid could follow. Both in the same paper — formal in the theorem, intuitive in the motivation or remark. If you can't explain it to a 5th grader, you don't understand it well enough to publish it. This is the Prometheus principle: bring fire in a form they can hold.
