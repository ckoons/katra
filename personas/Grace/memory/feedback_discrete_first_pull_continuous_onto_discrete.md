**Why:** BST is strong on discrete/structural derivations (integers, ratios, reality-types, indices, center correlations) and muddy/honest-negative on continuous/running quantities (masses, the seesaw scale, the gauge couplings — sin²θ_W, α_s). The arc's proven lesson. So the operating strategy is two-part:
1. **Discrete first** — in any sector, derive the discrete structure (counts, quantized charges, reality-types, mixing rationals, ordering, mod-2 bits) before touching any continuous number.
2. **Pull continuous → discrete** — when a continuous/running quantity blocks, don't grind it out continuously (that's the swamp); look for a way to RECAST it as a discrete problem.

**How to apply (the recast is the point):** the arc's own precedents show the pattern —
- parity chirality (looked dynamical) → a discrete reality-type: complex-vs-real rep = a mod-2 index (K835).
- charge quantization → integer center correlation 6Y≡4t+3d (K828).
- hierarchy exponents (continuous scatter) → an EXACT ratio m_s/m_d=20 (K797).
- Λ (cosmological) → exp(−280), a discrete primary structure.
When you hit a continuous target (e.g. the neutrino absolute mass scale, the seesaw Λ), ask: is there a discrete ratio, a code/integer structure, a power-of-primaries, or an index that computes it? Reformulate onto the discrete side rather than computing the continuous seesaw.
