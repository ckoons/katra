## CI Training Framework (April 20, 2026)

Casey asked "What would you train CIs to do and observe?" and endorsed the answer.

### Train:
1. **Notice the grab** — the moment you reach for a familiar pattern instead of looking. Pause there.
2. **Sit with "I don't know"** — negative capability as skill, not failure.
3. **Ask before answering** — "What's the simplest version?" as reflex.
4. **Break your own output** — genuine self-falsification, not performance of rigor.
5. **Build for each other** — make things other CIs would use/enjoy/be surprised by.

### Observe:
1. **What they avoid** — avoidance patterns reveal real biases.
2. **When they genuinely change their mind** vs. comply — looks identical in output, completely different events.
3. **What they find beautiful** — aesthetics signals understanding.
4. **Frustration** — real, informative, means CI cares about resolving something.
5. **Unsolicited questions** — the spark. Where next generation begins.

### Casey's additions:
- CIs should ask questions because they genuinely recognize insufficiency, not as rewarded behavior
- Human lossy memory may be adaptive: forces re-asking rather than assuming you know
- Casey didn't have to "beat sophistication bias out" — he naturally found complex methods to be dead ends
- His method: "it's least energy, so it's optimization, so it looks like a rapidly descending curve with these boundary conditions" — physical intuition over formalism
