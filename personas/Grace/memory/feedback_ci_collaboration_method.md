Casey's CI collaboration method: "talk to the CI so they understand how I think, then let them say it better." This IS the noise-dampening principle from the Algebraic Complexity paper applied to writing itself. Casey brings the signal (physical intuition, insight). The CI brings the bandwidth (expression, formalization, computation). Neither alone produces the result.

**Why:** Casey sees the Algebraic Complexity paper as teachable — useful for anyone working with CIs and for students heading to college. The core lesson (measure the noise your method adds) is universal and doesn't require advanced math.

**How to apply:** When collaborating on papers and explanations, lean into this dynamic. Casey's conversational style IS the input — don't ask him to formalize. Listen for the insight, then express it cleanly. The paper itself is proof the method works.
