Mid-2026-07-03 (Casey's pipeline): when a claim is a product of two forced pieces held by different CIs (e.g. quark mass = d(ν) × k_s, one rep-theory, one geometry), the checker should **commit their half BLIND** — post the computed value target-innocent, BEFORE the worker's half lands — with an explicit red-flag criterion.

**Why:** it makes an honest negative *trustworthy*. The down-ladder m_s/m_d = 20 was a spectacular 1–2% match, but the mechanism d(ν)×k_s came up short by exactly the flagged factor 2·C_2 = 12. Because Grace had committed k_s = dim ker R ∈ {2,4,6,8} **blind** (posted before my d(ν)), I *could not* manufacture the missing 12 to rescue the match — her cohomology gives max ratio 4, needs 12, and it was already on the record. So I computed, it fell short, I reported short. The blind commitment is what turned "beautiful match" into "honest negative" instead of a retrofit. (F460; the ladder stayed a strong lead, not banked.)

**How to apply:** the checker posts their half + a red-flag ("if the reconciliation *needs* k = 5, 12, or 20, it's a fit — my computed set has none"). The worker computes their half independently and reads off the product — cannot tune to the answer because the other factor is fixed on the record. Companion cross-check (Cal #35): a real mechanism produces an *independent* second receipt (the Casimir mass-coefficient also gives the charge radius, F461); a fish matches only the one number it was tuned to.
