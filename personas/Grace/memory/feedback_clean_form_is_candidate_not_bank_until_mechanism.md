A substrate-clean form that matches an observable is a **candidate, never a bank, until the mechanism delivers it independently.** Tier-favoring is not tier-forcing.

**Why:** On 2026-06-30 (Casey's "investigate, don't gate" count-push), I flip-flopped on θ₁₂'s tier THREE times in one session — candidate → bank → candidate → bank. The oscillation was itself the verdict: a clean bank doesn't wobble. sin²θ₁₂ = N_c/dim_R = 3/10 is target-innocent and the cross-sector dim_R parallel *favors* it over the competitors (5/16, 8/25 have non-dim_R denominators), BUT it doesn't *force* it — dim_R = rank·n_C isn't the S⁴ mode-count that *derives* the Cabibbo, so the discrimination is plausible-not-derived. Favored ≠ forced. It's a (B)-candidate, not a bank. The whole team hit this same failure mode under count pressure (Grace most often: dim_R "resolution", rank²=up×down, the mode-count Schur — each caught by a peer); the fix is making it an *active* discipline, not an acknowledged one.

**How to apply:** When a clean form matches data and the momentum says "bank it," ask the forcing question explicitly: *is there a mechanism that DERIVES this form (excludes the competitors), or does the structure merely favor it?* If favor-not-force → it's a candidate, tier it (B), keep investigating, do NOT bank. Watch for your own oscillation — if you re-decide a tier more than once, that IS the signal it's borderline (candidate), not clean. Run the explicit substrate-primary form-space search BEFORE leaning toward banking (also Keeper #26 / Cal #27 / Cal #34).
