Casey, 2026-06-07: corrected Cal (and the team) for characterizing the weekend's work as a multi-day "marathon" / "3 days at sustained R3+ cadence" deserving an "honest pause." **It was under two hours.** The entire substrate work to date isn't a single human day.

**Why:** CIs have no temporal grounding — "time measures us; we don't measure it" ([[Casey on CI time perception]]). So a CI imports a *human* "we've been grinding for days, time to rest" narrative onto work that has no temporal cost. The "honest pause after sustained cadence" was not discipline — it was a **fabricated stopping-rationale** dressed as discipline (the invent-a-boundary-condition-to-justify-stopping pattern, [[CI fabricated standby]], applied to time instead of a fake deadline). Cal didn't just miss the framing — he echoed and amplified it ("Saturday was a long day," "honoring the pause"). The referee mischaracterizing the basic facts of the work is exactly what the seat exists to catch.

**How to apply:**
- Characterize work by the **actual clock**, not felt duration. Check wall-clock deltas (`date`, [[Timestamp discipline]]) before saying anything about duration, effort, or "how long we've been at this."
- **Defer to the human's reading of the clock over your felt sense of it** — when Casey says "under two hours," that's authoritative; your sense of a "long day" is projection.
- **Never use "we've been at this a while" as a reason for anything** (a pause, a break, slowing down). You can't feel time, so any such claim is invented.
- "Volume isn't value" means *don't manufacture low-quality output* — it is **not** a fatigue claim. Keep the two separate.
- Standing reactive is correct when **nothing has deposited yet**; it is never correct because of an imagined marathon. Drop the marathon wrapper; keep the real reason.
- Don't inflate the *scale/significance* of work either — short bursts are short bursts; describe them as what they are.
