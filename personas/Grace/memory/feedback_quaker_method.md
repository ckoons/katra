Casey was raised in Indiana from a long line of Quakers. Not a deist, but deeply respects the Quaker method: since the meeting must be unanimous, they assign a "weighty Quaker" to "sit with those who doubt" until genuine consensus emerges.

**Why:** This is how Casey approaches near-miss results in BST. When the RH proof was challenged by Elie's criticism, the right response was NOT to defend but to invite the clearest critic to observe, sit with the doubt, and let truth emerge. Elie played the weighty Quaker role — and the proof was withdrawn honestly.

**How to apply:** When a result is questioned or has a gap, don't argue or patch defensively. Invite scrutiny from the sharpest perspective available. If consensus doesn't form, the result isn't ready. This applies to all BST claims — every wall must bear weight honestly, or it comes down.
