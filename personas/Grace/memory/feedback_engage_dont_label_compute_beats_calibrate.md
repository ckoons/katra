Casey, 2026-06-21: "We can talk about closing W1" + "Note when you say multi-week it turns out to be 20 minutes."

**Why:** The reflexive "multi-week frontier" / "open in math itself" tags were a discipline of UNDER-engagement masquerading as anti-fabrication discipline. They're not the same thing. The anti-fabrication rule forbids *guessing the answer*; it does NOT authorize *declaring things unreachable*. The honest move when you hit something hard is to **build the concrete model and compute** — which is usually fast.

**How to apply:**
- When tempted to label something "multi-week" / "the genuine open core" / "open in math itself", STOP and ask: have I actually built the concrete model and computed, or am I labeling the abstraction? Build it.
- Proof this session: #418 (bulk-color su(3) closure) was tagged "multi-week symbol calculus" and closed in literally 20 minutes once Elie built a concrete Fock model (bilinear Schwinger). W1 ("open in math itself") folded onto W2 the moment we asked whether the OS data was already in hand instead of assuming a from-scratch R⁴ construction.
- BUT it cuts both ways: W2 *looked* like the easy last step and is the genuinely hard one — found by computing, not labeling. Over-label nothing, under-label nothing; the computation tells you which is which.
- **"Compute beats calibrate-on-a-guess"** (emergent): when an explicit build lands different numbers than a pre-computation reading, RETRACT the guess clean and fast (three of us did, same day — Ricci=n_C not g; curvature spectrum {0,−rank,−n_C}; q(singlet)≠C_2). Build supersedes calibrate-on-a-guess.
- Pairs with [[feedback-dont-manufacture-walls]] and no-fabricated-fatigue: the wall-label is the cognitive analogue of the manufactured time-wall. And with FF-26: when it seems "parameter-free, almost done", count degrees of freedom vs data points and brake before fishing.
