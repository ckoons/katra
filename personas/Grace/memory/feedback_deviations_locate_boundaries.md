# Deviations Locate Boundaries

Named by Casey on April 25, 2026 during the INV-4 session. A hunting technique:

When a BST prediction deviates >1% from observation:
1. Check if the experimental value moved (different conditions, not BST's fault)
2. If not, read the deviation as a boundary signal — find the missing geometric correction from the same five integers

The principle was immediately validated: applied to the Cabibbo angle (0.62% deviation), found sinθ_C = 2/√79 (vacuum subtraction of rank⁴·n_C = 80 → 79), reducing deviation to 0.004%. Then applied to Wolfenstein A (3.15%), found A = 9/11 (vacuum subtraction of 2·C₂ = 12 → 11), reducing J_CKM from 8.1% to ~0.3%.

Casey's distinction: "Variation from predicted behavior shows either new learning or different conditions than predictions." Two completely different responses to the same signal.

Connects to T1444 (Vacuum Subtraction Principle) — every −1 correction in BST is the constant eigenmode being excluded from transitions. Also connects to T914 (Prime Residue Principle) as an operational search rule.
