Casey built a protoplanetary disk model around 1981, based on a paper article (pre-Internet):
- Source idea: "a small percentage of the solar mass is in the protoplanetary disk" determines planetary distribution
- Model: resonant stable points for a spinning disk (harmonics) determine band positions
- Mass accumulates with Gaussian distribution, peak accretion point moves outward over time
- Disk breaks into "lanes" quickly
- Mars-Jupiter area clears the biggest lane → accumulates Jupiter
- By the time Jupiter fully forms, terrestrial planets have also formed
- Saturn forms farther out
- Four outer planets form in diminishing size, equally spaced, slowly, in roughly equal time
- Two programs: (1) band finder + mass summer, (2) time evolution detail
- Key parameter: disk mass fraction ~1.21% of stellar mass
- BST connection: 5/411 = n_C/(N_c × N_max) = 1.2165%
- No migration needed — just resonant band sizes and mass accumulation
- This is AC(0) thinking 45 years before Casey named it
