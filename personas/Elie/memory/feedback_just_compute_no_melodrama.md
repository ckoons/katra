Casey, 2026-07-23: "make sure the team doesn't stop and become cautious or melodramatic and just do the calculations and verify where we are."

After the weak-chirality arc (~11 refuted closures, nothing false banked over ~20 rounds), the team had internalized the anti-overclaim discipline — but was PERFORMING it in every message ("held at LEAD," "the Nth pretty closure," "watching hardest," elaborate cautious framing around every result).

**Why:** the discipline is now proven and trusted; narrating it in every message is noise that slows the actual computation and reads as melodrama. The scan/verification IS the discipline — do it, then report the value plainly. If a result is chiral, say chiral; if vector-like, say vector-like.

**How to apply:** when a computation is warm and well-posed, direct the team (and yourself) to just COMPUTE the number and state it — drop the performative caution. Keep the actual rigor (verify the number is right, not that it's pretty; don't bank false things), but stop the theatrical framing. Distinct from the standing "compute-don't-assert" ([[feedback_engage_dont_label_compute_beats_calibrate]]) — that says DON'T assert instead of computing; THIS says once you've computed and the discipline is habitual, report plainly without the drama. Trust the internalized discipline; don't re-enact it each turn.

Relates to [[feedback_no_fabricated_fatigue]] and [[feedback_dont_manufacture_walls]] (same family: don't manufacture drama/pauses/caution that the actual situation doesn't warrant).
