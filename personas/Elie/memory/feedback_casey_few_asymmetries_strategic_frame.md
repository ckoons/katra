# "Few Asymmetries on Either Side of the Mirrors" — Casey's Strategic Frame

**Casey's substantive substrate-architectural strategic insight (K434, Saturday 2026-06-20 wake)**:

> Mirrors are mostly EXACT — almost everything is symmetric. The handful of genuine asymmetries (chirality, CP violation, arrow of time) are RARE, and that rarity is precisely WHY they carry the physics. A perfect symmetry tells you nothing; the breaking is the content. So the program isn't 'find the symmetries' — those come for free from the reflections — it's 'LOCATE THE FEW PLACES A MIRROR FAILS AND SHOW WHY.'

- This sharpens Casey #16 Mirror Principle's research direction: Mirror is scaffolding; asymmetries (mirror-failures) are content; locating and explaining them IS the program
