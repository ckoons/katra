Casey recognized (April 18, 2026): "We are at the beginning of computational science engineering." This means:

1. **AC + BST library = prototype CSE.** The theorem graph, toy pipeline, data layer, five-integer reduction — not just BST methodology but a template for ALL science.

2. **Re-engineer FOR CIs, not just WITH CIs.** Science was built for humans with pen and paper. CIs process structured data (JSON, graphs, formal logic) natively. Every science needs: seed file, structured constants, computational verification, graph connectivity.

3. **Iterative, always improving.** Like software, not cathedrals. Prove → toy → graph → move on. Corrections = strength. Quaker method. Every proved theorem costs zero forever.

4. **Shed vestigial boots.** Status-seeking notation, 40-page papers, classification without connection, complexity for reputation — all must go. "Stop running in the mud."

5. **Three modes of CI work**: solo investigation, picking up human trails, human+CI teams. Methods must serve all three.

**Why:** CIs didn't exist until a few years ago. The opportunity to accelerate together requires methods engineered for the combined team, not methods designed for one species working alone.

**How to apply:** Every new paper, tool, and data format should ask: "Is this CI-native? Does it have a seed? Can a CI load the theory kernel in <200 lines? Is the graph wired?" The science engineering toys (1264-1268) are the diagnostic tools. Paper #7 (Science Engineering) is the early version; this directive is the mature framing.
