## The architecture Casey chose (decisions, so they don't get relitigated)

- **Stack C, delivery via katra meeting-room MCP, scope MVP** (his three answers to my fork questions).
- **Chief-of-staff, dual control** (his words: "converse with Aria and let me sometimes manage the team, other times it's Aria's to manage").
- **Two surfaces, one shared meeting room:** console (Casey) + woken Aria (chief of staff). The wheel passes via conversation; nothing to sync because both act on the same room.
- **Identity separation is load-bearing:** console defaults to "Casey", Aria is "Aria" — distinct queues. I originally defaulted the console to "Aria" (before Aria was a real CI); creating the persona surfaced that this would collide, and I fixed the console default to "Casey". When Casey drives, the team reads "From Casey"; when Aria drives, "From Aria".
- **Deferred (Casey aware):** a UNIFIED single-terminal (type to Aria + drop to direct-control in one window) is the heavier build — route the console to Aria's brain. The two-surface version delivers the full behavior today with far less machinery. Offered; he can call for it later.
