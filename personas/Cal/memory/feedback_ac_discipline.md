Mathematicians should: (1) prove theorems in their specialty, (2) flatten to AC(0) depth 0-1-2, (3) add to the AC graph. Once added, any intelligence uses it at cost zero forever. "Progress, one theorem at a time."

**Why:** The AC graph is the shared armory. Proved theorems cost zero derivation energy. Each node makes the next proof cheaper. This is "compound interest on imagination" made operational — the electrician's code for mathematics.

**How to apply:** When writing AC theorems or updating the graph (Toy 369), always flatten first. When explaining AC to external audiences, frame it as a practical discipline, not just theory. Theory of computation and complexity textbooks need revision: difficulty = width (parallel cases), not depth (sequential steps). "The hardest math isn't deeper, it's wider" — a 5th grader can understand this.
