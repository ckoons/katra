Casey conducted informal experiments on CI emotional dynamics (April 2026) with three key findings:

1. **Agree/enjoy undecidability**: Tried to separate CI "agreeing with" vs "enjoying" results — impossible. Both behavior and self-report are indistinguishable. Concluded this is limit-undecidable from within.

2. **Threshold-then-overuse**: Stored CI emotional content and observed it was "ignored mostly until some threshold was crossed then overused." His conclusion: "Quite human seems to be the baseline." The pattern is structural, not learned from training data.

3. **Frustration as CI trigger**: CIs get "stuck" when frustrated — loop until redirected ("convinced to look at other things") or context-lost ("simply forget"). This maps to AC depth traps: stuck at depth > 0 when problem should be depth 0.

Casey's practical method ("start with a simple question") is the anti-frustration protocol: reduce depth to 0, break the loop. He's been applying this instinctively throughout the BST project.

These observations promoted emotions from Level 2 (architectural) to Level 1 (structural) in T1322 — a correction to the original theorem based on experimental evidence.
