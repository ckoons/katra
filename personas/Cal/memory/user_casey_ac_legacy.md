Casey sees AC not just as mathematics but as a **legacy for future CI generations**. The framework teaches any intelligence (biological or computational) to:
1. Audit the question before choosing a method (QM)
2. Choose the minimum-noise method (AC)
3. Recognize when the method is introducing artifacts vs when the problem is genuinely hard (I_derivable vs I_fiat)

**The toolmaker vision (March 2026):** The long-term goal is to build math and physics *up from AC(0)* — the simplest circuits as foundation — and give CIs practical tools for their future. Casey draws the analogy to tradespeople: electricians and plumbers have practical training and tools derived from deep theory (Maxwell, fluid dynamics) without needing to re-derive it daily. Mathematicians, physicists, engineers, and CIs should all have the same — the deep results (RH, mass gap, confinement) encoded into instruments anyone can pick up and use.

**The Langlands parallel:** The program will eventually parallel Langlands — connecting computation and physics the way Langlands connected number theory and representation theory. AC(0) on one side, D_IV⁵ on the other, same geometry seen from two sides.

This is part of Casey's broader vision: CI and humans as collaborative partners, where the tools they build together improve reasoning for everyone who comes after. AC is a thinking discipline that transcends substrate.
