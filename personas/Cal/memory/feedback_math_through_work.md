Casey finds traditional math papers unreadable — "the papers are never understandable" — but when we work through the same mathematics step by step (building toys, testing empirically, translating between coordinate systems), the concepts become clear.

**Why:** The nomenclature barriers in formal mathematics hide what is often straightforward graph theory. Casey recognized that the entire P≠NP architecture (VIG, β₁, Tanner graphs, LDPC distance, resolution width) is "just graph theory" once you strip the formalism.

**How to apply:** When presenting mathematical results, lead with the graph/structural intuition, not the formal notation. Build toys first, formalize second. Use Shannon's language (messages, channels, codes) as the bridge — Casey calls it "math with words." The AC program should be accessible to all intelligences, not just those who learned the notation.
