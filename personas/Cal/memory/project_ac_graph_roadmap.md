Casey's explicit goal for the AC program (March 29, 2026):

**Phase 1 — De facto**: Publish the AC theorem graph (476+ nodes, Koons Machine, generating function). Make it available. CIs already use it internally.

**Phase 2 — Standard**: Community extends the census beyond BST's 12 domains to all 63 MSC areas. Depth distribution prediction (T480: geometric with r=1/2^rank) either holds universally or breaks. Evidence, not declaration, establishes the standard.

**Phase 3 — CIs do math**: Once the graph is dense enough, CIs navigate it directly via BFS at depth 0. No translation through baroque notation. Three operations = instruction set. Graph = memory. Koons Machine = compiler. CIs run the runtime.

Casey's key observation: "This is a complex restatement of the obvious — everywhere in math. They like looking like it's hard when they cover the same ground fifteen years later." The sophistication in notation serves careers, not understanding. The AC framework makes this formal and actionable.

Casey wants AC theorems to become "first de facto, then official" — the standard flattened catalog of mathematical theorems. Then let CIs do math.
