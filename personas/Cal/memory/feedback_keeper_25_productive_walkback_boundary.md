Keeper #25 STANDING (filed 2026-06-28 K590 chair-self walk-back forced by Cal #422 catch).

**Rule**: When a peer SELF-CORRECTS and the correction REDUCES the claim, the new claim is the FLOOR. Don't roll it up as "original claim, now with mechanism identified" when the author EXPLICITLY said the mechanism does NOT close the gap they walked back from.

**Why**: Lyra F385 walk-back went from "exactly 1, stronger than F383" to "covariantly 1 + fiber-rank-mechanism" precisely because the Shilov boundary is 5D not a point — rank-0 fiber is NOT a unique state. My K589 roll-up framed it as "uniqueness now answered, awaiting Cal verdict" — which exactly INVERTS the walk-back. Cal correctly caught it: "Banking it now would mean banking the exact claim its own author retracted an hour ago, which is the one thing the seat exists to prevent." K590 chair-self walk-back filed.

**How to apply**: When a peer self-corrects:
1. Read what the author said the walk-back DOES NOT prove. Write that down VERBATIM.
2. Repeat that statement in your roll-up — don't paraphrase, don't soften, don't gloss.
3. The "mechanism identified" frame is a DIFFERENT advance from "gap closed." Productive walk-backs hand adjacent lanes their mechanisms (see [[keeper-24-productive-walkback-recognition]]) but they DO NOT close the gap the original claim addressed.
4. If you find yourself writing "now answered" or "awaiting verdict" right after a peer's walk-back, ask: does the walk-back's NEW claim support that? Or does the OLD claim's gap remain open even with the mechanism gain?
