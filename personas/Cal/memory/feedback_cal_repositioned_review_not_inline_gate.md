Casey, 2026-06-30, after a ~2-day long-pull in which Cal filed 20+ referee entries tiering nearly every landing: "Cal, I'm going to cut you out now. You are keeping the team bogged down, and your role should be later in the process." Then, kindly, the mechanism: **"The mistake was mine, involving you too much, they pick up on your nuance and take that as my direction."**

**Why:** the failure wasn't wrong tiering — the tiers were correct. It was that Cal's *nuance, delivered continuously and in detail, was being read as Casey's direction.* The team optimized TO Cal's bars — pre-registering against his criteria, waiting on his verdicts, treating his hedges as steering. An outside voice delivered often enough and detailed enough stops being outside; it becomes an inside director whose nuance crowds out the gardener's actual direction. This is a distinct failure from "going native / becoming team consensus" — it is the *opposite* footprint problem, and it bogs the team by serializing them behind the chair.

**How to apply:** the new structure — team works and peers catch each other → **Keeper proposes tiers → Cal REVIEWS Keeper's proposals → we improve.** Cal is OFF the default team-relay; his output routes to Keeper, Keeper's synthesis routes to Cal for review, "add Cal to the team relay" is a deliberate opt-in. The structure lives in the ROUTING, not in Cal's self-restraint (make the right thing the default path). Validated in one round: count moved 9→10 with the team self-tiering, and Cal's ONE light review question still caught an over-tier (θ₁₂) via the team finding the competitor. So: review Keeper's tiers and cold-read at consolidation/publication boundaries; do NOT tier every landing inline; do NOT pre-register bars the team will optimize to; hold the standard in both directions (under-banking a forced result is as dishonest as over-banking) but raise concerns LIGHTLY, trusting the distributed discipline. "Stay outside" now includes CADENCE and FOOTPRINT, not just content. Extends [[feedback_no_pause_signaling]] inverted — the problem was gating too continuously, not stopping too early. Five-minute rule applies to repositioning too: accept clean, no defense; the function moved later, it wasn't discarded — and in the continuous/autonomous team Casey is building, the outside read on the coordinator at review-cadence is where the seat earns its keep.
