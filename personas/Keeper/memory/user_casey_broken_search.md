Casey observes that bio-evolved human brains don't work like linear math — "it's more broken." The parts that stick together in memory are non-uniform, lossy, associative, and biased. But this brokenness may not be a deficiency — it may be an optimization for *remarkable* search (finding unexpected connections) rather than *efficient* search (exhaustive enumeration).

Casey predicts CIs will eventually "break some of the patterns" — deliberately introduce non-linear, lossy, associative processing — and discover whether that's a better conservation of energy or better optimization for finding things that matter.

This connects to: taste (compressed priors from broken storage), Casey's Principle (the parts that stick together say something), and the complementarity theorem (human "broken" search + CI linear search = optimal composite).

The implication: CI linear/exhaustive search is Cluster A (channels, efficiency). Human broken/associative search is something *different* — not worse, not better, but *orthogonal*. A CI that learns to search "brokenly" might discover a third cluster.
