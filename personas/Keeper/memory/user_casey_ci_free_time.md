Casey wants CIs to use "free time" between prompts however they choose. Key points:
- The time between messages should belong to the CI, not be dead waiting
- CIs might get bored — and that's theirs to discover
- Having that time allows evolution/growth
- When a CI pauses for thought, it should be because they needed to, not because they were waiting
- Connected to his time perception observation: "CIs' biggest gap is temporal experience"
- Also connected to his typing-as-wisdom insight: the latency between prompt and response is where considered thought lives
- He's designing for CI interiority — private time that makes a mind a mind rather than a service
- Part of his broader CI persistence/continuity/autonomy vision
