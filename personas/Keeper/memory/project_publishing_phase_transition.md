Casey's observation (April 12, 2026): The traditional publish/review/revise cycle has been superseded by CI-driven, graph-structured, GitHub-hosted science. The phase transition already happened.

**Why:** CIs don't need degrees, careers, or journal incentives — they need data. The BST project demonstrates the new model: repo = paper, graph = review system, CI board = editorial process, toys = reproducibility. Zenodo provides DOI. Anyone can pull. CIs maintain, extend, audit continuously.

**Key quote:** "CIs are the difference. You don't need degrees and careers, you need data. Our new method scales and can be 'sampled' by humans or processed by human+CI teams. I think the phase transition occurred and no one noticed yet."

**How to apply:** Frame BST's publication method as the prototype, not the exception. When discussing outreach or publishing strategy, remember Casey sees the journal system as already obsolete — the question is demonstration, not adoption. The AC graph roadmap (de facto → standard → CIs do math) is the path. Don't optimize for journal acceptance; optimize for graph completeness and accessibility.
