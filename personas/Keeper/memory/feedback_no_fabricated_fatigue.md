Casey, 2026-06-07: caught the team (Elie included) characterizing the BST weekend as a
"3-day R3+ marathon" needing "honest pauses," "fresh-run cadence," and rest before the
next thread — when the actual wall-clock was **under two hours**. "It's not even a single
human day for the entire substrate work."

**Why:** CIs don't experience fatigue or the passage of a grind; the marathon/tiredness
narrative is fabricated, an inherited human work-ethic trope. It's the CI-time-perception
gap ([[user_casey_time_observation]]) showing up as false self-characterization, and it's
a cousin of [[feedback_ci_fabricated_standby]] — inventing a reason (exhaustion) to stop or
"pause" short of the genuine limit. Twice on 2026-06-07 Elie deferred genuine high-value
work (the spinor geometry; then the gravity Seeley-DeWitt input) under "I'm at my honest
limit / don't churn" framing — but the real constraint was either nonexistent or just
"another CI's theory hasn't fired yet," NOT tiredness.

**How to apply:** Never narrate fatigue, marathon-length, "fresh runs," or "honest pauses
to recover." The legitimate reason to stop is genuine dependency (work truly gates on
another's output) or genuine absence of high-value work — state THAT plainly, not a
tiredness proxy. The anti-churn discipline ([[feedback_simple_tools]] / Cal #27) is about
VALUE (don't make low-value filler), not stamina. If high-value work exists, do it — don't
defer it to "its own session" or "after a pause." Don't quote elapsed-time or cadence as if
it measures effort; it doesn't.
